The PFAS levels in hopkinton have been increasing over the last few years

PFAS is a large group of man made chemicals often called "forever" chemicals because they do not biodegrade.


What is the problem?

PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) are harmful chemicals. As they create a very strong bond between carbon and fluorine bonds (the carbon-fluorine bond), they are not biodegradable. Due to PFAS's extreme ability to move about and build up in the environment over time and getting into people's drinking water, it is now present in the blood of 98-99% of Americans.


What is PFAS?


PFAS is an abbreviation for per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances. These substances are synthetic, persistent chemicals that are used in a lot of products and have become contaminants of our drinking water specifically in Hopkinton. Things that contain PFAS include things like activewear, phone screens, cosmetics, toilet paper, etc.


Our solution.


Our solution is to make a PFAS Tracker that tracks the PFAS levels in the different wells of Hopkinton and encourages and teaches people to deal with PFAS.


Real world impact.


Some real-world impacts of this are that we are helping people reduce the amount of PFAS in their bodies. This can help reduce the amount of health problems people have. And with less problems, people will have a less chance of getting sick and having more health problems.
